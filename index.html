The Problem

Airlines and MRO operators routinely make capital allocation decisions based on maintenance liability data that is never audited at the record level. Cancelled flights get counted as completed maintenance cycles. Ghost aircraft accumulate real wear with no registry tracking. The result is a financial model built on dirty data — and no one knows by how much.

This analysis forensically audits 7.07 million BTS flight records against the FAA Master Registry to answer one question: what is the actual, verified maintenance liability of the US domestic fleet?

Two distinct failure modes drive the exposure. Phantom liability — where cancelled flights incorrectly accrue maintenance costs — inflates the numbers lessors use for reserve calculations. Hidden liability — where aircraft operate outside the official registry — generates real hardware degradation that no reserve fund is tracking.

$17.3M Phantom Liability Eliminated

96,315 cancelled flights were incorrectly accruing a $180 per-cycle maintenance charge — generating liability that never had a physical basis. The pipeline corrects this at source.

Primary Findings

Finding 1: Phantom Liability. The standard MRL formula applies $180 per cycle to every flight record uniformly. Initial processing revealed 96,315 cancelled flights generating $17,336,700 in phantom maintenance liability. The pipeline enforces a hard-zero on cancelled flights before any cost accrues.

Finding 2: Ghost Aircraft. A cross-reference between BTS flight logs and the FAA Master Registry returned 314 aircraft with no official match. After full tail number standardization, 199 confirmed Ghost Aircraft remained — actively accumulating hours with no reserve contribution being tracked.

Finding 3: Carrier Controllability. 34.5% of all delay minutes are directly carrier-controllable — attributable to the airline's own operations, not weather, ATC, or security. This is the core accountability metric for operational intervention.

88.9% Technical Dispatch Reliability

Percentage of total flights with no carrier-attributable delay. Computed from 7.07M records. TDR Score: the benchmark that operations teams track against IATA industry standards.

Finding 4: Geographic Concentration. Maintenance liability clusters in legacy hub states — Texas ($1.02B), Florida ($885M), California ($757M) — independently of flight volume. This points to station-level infrastructure age, not purely operational scale.

Finding 5: Liability Concentration. The top 20% of the fleet generates approximately 80% of all maintenance delay cost. Generalized fleet upgrades are a capital misallocation. The analytical output isolates the exact tail numbers requiring immediate intervention.

199 Aircraft Flying Without Official Registry — The Forensic Method

The Ghost Aircraft Reconciliation

A LEFT JOIN between 7.07 million BTS flight records and the FAA Master Registry initially returned 314 aircraft with no official registry match. These aircraft were operating — accumulating flight hours and landing cycles, contributing to physical wear — but did not exist in the official record.

The root cause was not fraud. It was data. Inconsistent N-prefix formatting between the BTS dataset and FAA registry caused legitimate aircraft to appear unregistered. After applying tail number standardization across both datasets, 115 of those 314 resolved cleanly.

199 confirmed Ghost Aircraft remained. These cannot be explained by formatting. They represent genuine registry gaps: registration lapsed without renewal, ownership transfer paperwork incomplete, or aircraft deregistered pending export but still operating in the network.

The maintenance reserve contributions an operator owes a lessor are calculated from official registry records. If the aircraft is invisible in the registry, those hours generate no reserve contribution. The physical wear happens. The payment does not. That gap is the lessor's unprotected exposure at redelivery.

The Technical Solution: Medallion Pipeline

The pipeline follows a three-layer Medallion architecture — separating raw ingestion, transformation, and delivery. Each tool does only what it does best. DuckDB handles the heavy transformation. Power BI handles the display. Neither is asked to do the other's job.

Bronze Layer
Raw Ingestion
  • 12 monthly BTS ZIP files from BTS.gov
  • FAA MASTER.txt — official aircraft registry
  • ACFTREF.txt — manufacturer reference data
  • ~11GB raw input, zero schema failures
  • Retry logic on network failures
Silver Layer
DuckDB Vectorized ETL
  • N-prefix CASE standardisation on tail numbers
  • MRL liability per flight (cancelled = $0)
  • LEFT JOIN ghost aircraft detection
  • UNPIVOT delay columns → 2.35M rows
  • ROW_NUMBER() surrogate keys generated
Gold Layer
Power BI Delivery
  • Aviation_Fact_Table — 7.07M rows
  • Aviation_Fact_Delay_Table — 2.35M rows
  • Master_Dim — 5,913 verified aircraft
  • Ghost_Aircraft_Audit — 199 records
  • 6x SNAPPY Parquet: 350MB → 96MB

Architecture decision: DuckDB over Pandas. At 11GB, a standard Pandas DataFrame exceeds available RAM and crashes. DuckDB's columnar vectorized engine processes data in memory-efficient SIMD chunks — aggregating millions of rows per CPU clock cycle without loading the full dataset into RAM.

Core Pipeline Code

Silver Layer — MRL Formula · $17.3M Phantom Liability Eliminated
-- 96,315 cancelled flights × $180 = $17,336,700 eliminated at source
CASE
    WHEN TRY_CAST(Cancelled AS INTEGER) = 1
      OR TRY_CAST(AirTime AS INTEGER) IS NULL THEN 0.0
    ELSE ((TRY_CAST(AirTime AS INTEGER) / 60.0) * 250.0) + 180.0
END AS MRL_Liability
Audit Layer — Ghost Aircraft Detection · Set Difference
# active_fleet - registered = orphans with no FAA registry match
active_fleet = set(fact_tails['Tail_Number'].dropna().apply(clean_tail))
registered   = set(merged_master['N-NUMBER_CLEAN'].dropna())
orphans = active_fleet - registered  # 199 confirmed ghost aircraft

Dynamic Risk Classification — The Parametric Threshold Engine

How the Kill Zone Works

The Kill Zone is engineered — not designed. The color of every dot in the scatter plot is computed in real time by a DAX measure that evaluates each aircraft's total MRL liability against the user-controlled threshold. When the slicer moves, the DAX context propagates across all 7.07 million records and redraws the entire visual in under a second.

This is not a filter. A filter would simply hide rows. This is a live DAX parameter changing the color logic, the Priority Table ranking, and the at-risk count simultaneously across every visual on the page — driven by a disconnected parameter table built with GENERATESERIES and SELECTEDVALUE.

Header-Detail Schema — 40+ Custom DAX Measures Powering Live Analytics

The Semantic Layer

The Power BI semantic model is a Header-Detail Schema — one header fact tables, one detail fact table, joined to five dimension tables, with a disconnected parameter table driving the Kill Zone threshold. Surrogate integer keys (Flight_ID) replace high-cardinality text tail numbers across all relationships, maximizing VertiPaq columnar compression.

Header-Detail Schema. Fact_Flights (7.07M rows) connects to Dim_Date (1:*), Dim_Airlines (1:*), Dim_Geo_Origin (1:*), Dim_Geo_Dest (1:*), and Master_Dim (1:*). Fact_Delays (2.35M rows, unpivoted delay breakdown) connects via Flight_ID (*:1 from Fact_Flights). MRL_Budget_Slicer is a disconnected parameter table — no relationship — providing the dynamic threshold value via SELECTEDVALUE in DAX. Ghost_Aircraft_Audit (199 records) maintained as a standalone audit table.

The DAX Analytical Engine

40+ custom DAX measures power the dashboard's analytical depth. Five that define the project's core analytical capability:

Measure Analytical Function
[Scatter Color Logic] Dynamic color classification driving the Kill Zone. Evaluates [True Total MRL Liability] > [MRL_Budget_Slicer Value] per aircraft — returns #D92525 (red) or #114477 (navy) on every threshold change across 7M records in real time.
[Months to Inflection] Forward-looking risk signal. Calculates months remaining before cumulative MRL accrual burns through the strategic reserve, based on current 30-day burn rate. A predictive metric, not a rear-view one.
[Controllability Ratio] Isolates carrier-controllable delay cost from external delay cost (weather, NAS, security). Produces the 34.5% accountability figure — the metric that determines which delays the operator is operationally responsible for.
[Liability Burn Rate
(per flight hour)]		 MRL cost accruing per flight hour for any selected aircraft or context. Surfaces which assets are degrading fastest relative to their utilization — displayed live in the drill-through header.
[Status Icon SVG] Generates an SVG warning triangle (red) or checkmark (green) directly in DAX — no custom visual required. Evaluates live MRL vs. threshold per aircraft and returns the appropriate icon URI string for the card visual.
